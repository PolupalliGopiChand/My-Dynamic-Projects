Varanasi

The city is very commonly referred to as City of Temples, Holy City of India, Religious Capital of India, and City of Learning.

Varanasi is one of the oldest living cities in the world. Its prominence in Hindu mythology is virtually unparalleled. Mark Twain, the English author, once wrote, ''Benaras is older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend and looks twice as old as all of them put together".

October to March is the best time to visit Varanasi. During this period, the fairs are held, starting from Diwali and continuing to Dev Diwali, which is celebrated on the 15th day after Diwali. The ghats are adorned with lights and diyas, making it an unforgettable sight.

Some must-see attractions include Dashashwamedh Ghat and the holy River Ganges. Dashashwamedh Ghat is one of the oldest and most important ghats, leading to the Ganges, near the Vishwanath temple.
